## Mission, Vision and Background

### Background

Kwekwe Polytechnic originated as a training college built by **Union Carbide (Pvt) Ltd**, now **ZIMASCO**, to develop technical manpower.  The college was donated to the Government of Zimbabwe in **1981**.  Initial intakes offered **Automotive, Electrical and Mechanical Engineering** programmes.  The **Commerce Division** opened in **1984**, **Adult and Continuing Education Division** in **2001**, and degrees were introduced in **2003**【208314490489181†L54-L67】.

### Mission

The institution’s mission is **to provide quality technical, vocational and technopreneurial education and training that is employable**【208314490489181†L77-L79】.

### Vision

Its vision is to be a **beacon in technical and manufacturing technology education** that is relevant for industry, supports entrepreneurship and promotes development【208314490489181†L84-L85】.

### Values

The core values include **Inquiry**, **Integrity**, **Initiative**, **Ingenuity**, **Improvement** and **Interplay**【208314490489181†L91-L97】.

### Strategic Outlook

In the Principal’s welcome note, **Mr E. Musara** states that Kwekwe Polytechnic follows a **five‑year strategic plan (2021‑2025)** aligned with national development strategies.  The institution applies a **heritage‑based Education 5.0 philosophy** anchored on **teaching, research, community service, innovation and industrialisation**【911913627917937†L56-L90】.  The plan supports the **National Development Strategy 1 (NDS1)** and **Vision 2030** goals for Zimbabwe, emphasising **human capital development and innovation** as drivers of a knowledge‑based economy【911913627917937†L65-L83】.
